Arenas

  • The building, which formerly hosted bull races in the city, is today a spectacular commercial center that, in addition to conserving the original building, has recovered the roof, which offers privileged views of the Montjuïc park and the city of Barcelona. A center of the old bullring of the Plaza de España, las Arenas, is currently one of the centers most important and visited commercial centers in the city both by the locals themselves and tourists. This bullring was made in the year 1900, built by the architect August Font i Carreras ..

  • Apart from the fight, the square has witnessed many events throughout the history of Barcelona, since during the Civil War it became headquarters of the republican side. In 1977 the place celebrated its last race with the participation of the bullfighter José Manuel Dominguín, among others. After the latter, the square fell into disuse until, in 2007, the decision was made to transform it into a commercial center.

    • The architects Richard Rogers and Alfonso Balaguer decided maintain the old facade, and added a dome-shaped roof that can be climbed and from which you can climb Enjoy one of the best views of Barcelona.

    This center has shops, movie theaters, restaurants and all kinds of services that, in combination with the beauty that still conserves the place, they have turned it into a very frequented point

    Splau

  • It is a shopping center opened in 2010 located between Cornellà de Llobregat and El Prat de Llobregat, next to the stadium of Espanyol. It was inaugurated on May 11, 2010. The center has 33,100 square meters of surface area for rent, 148 shops and 2,800 parking spaces. The shops are grouped in a ring of two floors and has establishments of large chains such as Mercadona, MediaMarkt, Pull and Bear, etc.
